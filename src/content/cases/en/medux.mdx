## The challenge

Medux, a leading Dutch healthcare organization for people with mobility disabilities, faced a challenge. Their customer base consists largely of elderly individuals who prefer calling over online booking. With a growing number of clients, scaling customer service without exploding costs became increasingly difficult.

A chatbot wasn't an option: many customers aren't comfortable with digital interfaces. Medux needed a solution that respects human contact while staying scalable and cost-efficient.

  </ul>

## The solution

We developed a voice-to-voice AI solution that can conduct natural phone conversations. The system sounds human, understands context, and can independently schedule appointments, answer questions, and process forms.

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-6 mb-8 not-prose">
  <div class="p-6 bg-white border border-stone-200"><h4 class="font-bold text-brand-dark mb-2">Natural conversations</h4><p class="text-sm text-stone-600">AI that sounds human and understands context. Customers often don't realize they're talking to a system.</p></div>
  <div class="p-6 bg-white border border-stone-200"><h4 class="font-bold text-brand-dark mb-2">Multilingual</h4><p class="text-sm text-stone-600">Support for multiple languages, eliminating language barriers.</p></div>
  <div class="p-6 bg-white border border-stone-200"><h4 class="font-bold text-brand-dark mb-2">Smart handoffs</h4><p class="text-sm text-stone-600">Complex or urgent questions are automatically transferred to human agents.</p></div>
