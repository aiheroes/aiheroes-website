"Is there actually a European alternative to ChatGPT?" is a question we get weekly. The answer is yes, with nuance. This overview lists the serious options per category, with the honest caveats attached.

## Why consider an alternative

The short version: data at American providers falls under the CLOUD Act regardless of server location, and the <a href="/en/resources/eu-ai-act-compliance">EU AI Act</a> asks for transparency that is easier to deliver on European or self-hosted infrastructure. The full reasoning is in our article on <a href="/en/resources/sovereign-ai">sovereign AI</a>.

## Category 1: European AI assistants (SaaS)

- **Mistral / Le Chat (France).** The most complete European alternative: a capable chat assistant, strong models and business plans. For most office applications a direct ChatGPT replacement.
- **Aleph Alpha (Germany).** Focused on government and regulated sectors, with an emphasis on explainability and sovereign deployment.
- **Proton Lumo (Switzerland).** Privacy-first assistant from the makers of Proton Mail; strong where confidentiality outweighs feature breadth.

The practical test: have your team do its own tasks in a European assistant for a week. The gap almost always turns out smaller than expected.

## Category 2: open models in European cloud

Open(-source) models, including Mistral's models and European initiatives like EuroLLM, run with European cloud providers such as OVHcloud, Scaleway, IONOS or Hetzner. You get modern model quality while data stays in the EU under EU law. This is the route we implement most often for organisations that want more than SaaS but no server room.

## Category 3: on-premises

For the most sensitive processes, compact open models now run fine on your own hardware. Maximum control, no data leaving the building. Since small models became strong, this fits SME budgets too; see our <a href="/en/services/eu-development">EU Development approach</a>.

## The honest caveat

If you are used to the absolute frontier of American models, you will notice a difference on some tasks. The relevant question is not "what is the best model in the world" but "what does this application need". For the vast majority of business AI use, summarising, writing, customer questions, document processing, that difference is negligible in 2026; for a few specialist tasks it is not. Making that distinction is exactly the work.
