## Where the money goes

### Training: EUR 2,500 to 4,000 per day

<a href="/en/services/incompany-ai-training">In-company AI training</a> is the cheapest investment with the broadest effect: your whole team works faster and more safely with AI afterwards. The price varies with group size and customisation. Compare that with the hidden cost of not training: employees reinventing every wheel, or pasting company data into public tools.

### Consulting: EUR 3,000 to 50,000 and beyond

At the lower end sits the <a href="/en/services/consulting#ai-readiness-scan">AI readiness assessment</a> (from EUR 3,000): in 1 to 3 days you know where you stand and what the logical next step is. A full <a href="/en/services/consulting#ai-roadmap">AI roadmap</a> with quantified business cases or long-running implementation guidance sits in the tens of thousands. Rule of thumb: the bigger the decision that follows, the more the groundwork may cost.

### Software: EUR 15,000 to 100,000 and beyond

<a href="/en/services/software#custom-ai-solutions">Custom AI software</a> has the widest spread. A well-scoped application, like automating one document flow, sits at the lower end. Systems with multiple integrations, high accuracy requirements or heavy compliance sit at the top. We almost always start with a proof of concept: a working prototype in days (at <a href="/en/cases/trabu">Trabu</a>, 6 days) before committing to the full build.

## The costs nobody budgets

Three items that are often missing from quotes but decide the business case:

- **Data preparation.** If your data is scattered, polluted or unstructured, time goes there before AI can do anything with it.
- **Adoption.** A working system nobody uses returns nothing. Reserve budget for training and change management.
- **Maintenance.** Models, integrations and regulation keep changing. Count on an annual amount for upkeep and iteration.

## What does it return?

Costs without returns mean nothing. At <a href="/en/cases/medux">Medux</a>, a custom AI solution delivered a 70% cost reduction on a labour-intensive process. Not every project hits numbers like that; that is why we quantify the business case before building, and sometimes advise not to build at all.
